Parrish Family Update 2025

For those who enjoy a long-form update, here is ours. We hope you enjoy reading it as much as we enjoyed living it.

We moved to the farm in August 2023 and named it To The Moon. At the time, Poppy hadn’t been born yet—we weren’t married, had one dog, one cat, and two horses. After the frenzy of moving, we settled into nesting mode, preparing for Poppy’s home birth. While Eva focused on rest and letting her body pour itself into growing Poppy, Jacob learned the honest, grounding labor that comes with caring for horses.

Poppy arrived right on her due date, January 6th, 2024. She is pure joy—determined, observant, playful, deeply caring, and endlessly curious about animals. She turns two in just a couple of weeks, which somehow feels impossible.

Riding the high of Poppy’s birth, we made what felt like a slightly unhinged but ultimately wonderful decision: two weeks later, we added another member to the family. We already had one baby crying—why not two? Rocket is a character: smart, energetic, cuddly, and incredibly gentle with Poppy. He somehow manages to be everywhere at once, always keeping tabs on everyone across the farm. Other than eating, his favorite activity is darting through the woods.

During these first couple years at the farm, Jacob learned what it really means to be with a horse woman - more horses! We had a slew of client horses come through, but have now settled in on our own horse family. Each has their own distinct personality and presence.

The addition that truly made it feel like a farm, though, was Deh and his hens. Poppy named the rooster “Deh” because it was one of the only words she could say at the time. The chickens roam freely, wreaking havoc on the flower beds, making hilarious noises, and leaving eggs for us to discover. If you don’t have chickens yet, consider this your sign—they’re easy, endlessly entertaining, and the eggs are unbeatable.

In September, we got married beneath a canopy of redwoods in Big Sur with our parents by our sides. It was a powerful, intimate, and enchanting week filled with love and more happy tears than we can count.

About a month later, we hosted a dinner party at the farm to celebrate with friends. We gathered in the barn and out on a deck in the far field. Despite the blistering cold, it was a beautiful celebration of love, food, friendship, and creative expression.

Jacob continues to run Bitcoin Charlotte remotely and hosted nearly 100 events throughout 2025. It’s been so energizing to see the community deepen and grow. If you still don’t own any bitcoin, take this as your sign—reach out to Jacob and get some or learn more at BitcoinCharlotte.org.

Eva has also been busy—she’s started showing and fully stepped into her journey toward becoming a top-tier hunter rider. Next year, our family is embracing the road. We’ll spend much of the year showing in Venice, FL; Traverse City, MI; Blowing Rock, NC; and close to home at Tryon, NC. We’re excited to live the RV life for months at a time, explore new places, and spend time with friends and family along the way.

Thank you so much for reading and for being part of our lives from near or far. We wish you and yours the very best this holiday season.

With love,
The Parrishes
